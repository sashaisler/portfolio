Mini Musical Chairs

What is it?

Mini Musical Chairs is a game that was created by me and a team of three other people for a class assignment. We were given the semester to create a game using an Arduino and showcase our project at an expo at the conclusion of the semester. Our project is a variation on musical chairs that allows for the game to be played with your fingers. My contribution was writing the code, which was done in the Arduino IDE using C++, the wiring, CADing, and 3D Printing, in addition to aiding in the overall assembly of the project and contributing to the presentation at the expo.

Github Project: https://github.com/sisler101/Mini-Musical-Chairs

Components:

The project included: Two Arduinos (used two to solve power issues that arose from having many components) LCD Display Three buttons (add, subtract, enter) Speaker LED Strip Four Servo Motors Five 3D printed chairs Four 3D printed connectors to attach the servo motors to the chairs Laser Cut box made from wood

ToSendList

What is it?

ToSend List is a climbing themed to do list I created using HTML and JavaScript. It is currently published on the Chrome Web Store and is available to add to your Chrome for free. Not only does it have the basic functionalities of a to do list (add, delete, and mark tasks as complete), it also allows you to grade your tasks on the bouldering scale (supports both V-Scale and Font-Scale).

Motivation:

This project was inspired by two of my obsessions: rock climbing and to do lists. I have been rock climbing for 6 years now and even competed on a youth team when I was younger. I am also always jotting things down on to do lists, whether in my reminders app, notes app, or on a post-it. To do lists are the only way I manage to stay organized and on top of my tasks. I realized, why not combine my passion for climbing with my favorite productivity tool? And thats when ToSend List was born.
